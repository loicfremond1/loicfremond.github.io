Loïc Frémond

I work across strategic communications, venture capital, and civic technology — usually at the point where politics, trade, and technology meet.

I'm a venture partner at Verb Ventures, an early-stage fund investing in B2B marketplaces and global trade infrastructure. I also co-founded DearMP, a parliamentary casework platform built to cut the email processing burden on MPs' offices in half. Before that I worked in Parliament, which is where the idea came from.

I write about politics, technology, and political economy for UnHerd, and publish Folgate Signals, a Substack on venture capital and politics. I'm interested in institutional design, industrial policy, and the way technology reshapes the structures people actually live inside — parliaments, supply chains, energy systems, cities.

I run the London Schvitz Club, a curated sauna community for founders, investors, and people building in AI. It started as an experiment in whether you could build a better network by slowing down, and it turns out you can.

I'm also involved in Athelstan 1100, a project to create a national monument to King Æthelstan — the first king of all England — to mark the 1100th anniversary of England's founding in 927. Privately funded, classically sculpted, and intended as an act of remembrance linking England's constitutional origins to the wider English-speaking world.
